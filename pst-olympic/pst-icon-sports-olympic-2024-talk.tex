\slideTitle{Challenges, Scope, Objectives and Outcomes}
\begin{itemize}[itemsep=4mm]\LARGE
\item Key Challenges and Problem Statement
\begin{itemize}[before=\large]
\item Holistic approaches for effective visual designing of art
\end{itemize}

\item Scope: Traditional and Emerging Design Principles
\begin{itemize}[before=\large]
\item Classical art and industrial design concepts
\item Persistent digital design concepts
\end{itemize}
\item Specific Objectives
\begin{itemize}[before=\Large]
\item Design Using Vector Graphics
\begin{itemize}[nosep,before=\normalfont\large]
\item Develop integrated and harmonised approach
\item Demonstrate a system-wide whole system approach in any operational environment and print media
\item Explore synergies between the mathematics of art and art of mathematics
\end{itemize}
\item Digital Tools
\begin{itemize}[nosep,before=\normalfont\large]
\item Manage bigdata: secured storage and open access
\item For the screen using \href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SVG}{Scalable Vector Graphics}
\item For the screen and print using \href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PostScript}{PostScript}, \href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TeX}{\TeX}, \href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LaTeX}{\LaTeX} and friends
\end{itemize}
\item Capacity Building - Knowledge and Technology Transfer
\end{itemize}
\item Expected Outcomes
\begin{itemize}[before=\large]
\item Develop dynamic digital tools for screen and print media
\item Guidance for adaptation and policy making
\item Digital Tools for Holistic Design: Digital Twin, Data-driven, and Model-based solutions
\end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\slideTitle{Project Aims, Objectives and Outcomes}
\begin{itemize}[before=\LARGE\normalfont,itemsep=6mm]
\item Aims
\begin{itemize}[before=\large]
\item Provide open data platform for data exchange and research innovation that is collaborative, interactive, secured, and open access
\item Provide digital tools powered by intelligent algorithms and reliable services needed to enable and scale-up research and innovation in the field
\item Provide holistic water quality monitoring system for traditional and emerging contaminants using direct methods and indirect correlational techniques
\item Implement and demonstrate water monitoring and hierarchical decision support system that is driven by the needs of its users/stakeholders at TRL 1-6
\end{itemize}
\item Specific Objectives
\begin{itemize}[before=\large]
\item Monitor the water quality of the city/region of Prague as a part of the catchment area of the River Labe/Elbe and underlined urban water networks
\item Provide a holistic monitoring of 50 types of Persistent Organic Pollutants (POPs), 25 types of Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFASs), 5 types of active pharmaceutical ingredients, and emerging microbial contaminants, pathogens or antimicrobial resistance genes as substances of very high concern
\item Develop integrated framework for geospatial Earth observations, physical sensors, chemical/biological analyses, and socioeconomic market intelligence
\item Provide a unified framework for long-term access and preservation of digital(ised) data, and a cloud platform ecosystem with built-in tools and services% that are collaborative, interactive, secured, and open access
\item Establish a local, regional, transnational and pan-European water quality policy guidelines from multiple perspectives for multiple stakeholders
\end{itemize}
\item Expected Outcomes
\begin{itemize}[before=\large]
\item Enhanced ability of researchers/stakeholders to interact across disciplinary, institutional, sectorial and political boundaries and cooperate effectively in advancing water quality monitoring and in developing innovative digital solutions for the analyses, valorisation and informed decision/policy-making
\item Enhanced ability to apply integrated monitoring techniques using conventional and emerging sensors, and bigdata market intelligence within the study area that can be easily extended to other water ecosystems in Europe (and beyond)
\item Valorised intellectual property as novel monitoring techniques, digital twins, and decision support tools to unleash the full potential of a digitally enabled water monitoring  ecosystem connecting rivers, streams, and industrial and residential sewerage networks
\end{itemize}
\end{itemize}
